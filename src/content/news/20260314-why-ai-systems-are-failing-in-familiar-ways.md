A New Stack analysis argues that many current AI failures look a lot like older software failures: weak requirements, brittle integrations, poor observability, and overconfident rollout plans.

The broader significance is that software organizations are moving from experimentation to operational decisions about how AI should be embedded in products, tooling, and team processes.
