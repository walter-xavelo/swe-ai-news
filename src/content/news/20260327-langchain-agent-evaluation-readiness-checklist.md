## Overview

LangChain published an evaluation-readiness checklist for agents, effectively turning a messy practice into a more explicit operational discipline. The checklist framed eval maturity as something teams can prepare for systematically instead of improvising after failures.

## Why It Matters For SWE

As agents get deployed beyond toy tasks, teams need a structured way to decide whether they are actually ready for production. Checklists are boring, but boring is what reliable engineering looks like at scale.

## Key Signals

- Agent eval maturity is becoming a management and engineering concern.
- Teams want clearer production-readiness gates for agent systems.
- The ecosystem is codifying best practices around reliability.
- AI products increasingly resemble normal software operations disciplines.

## SWE Industry Takeaway

The more serious the deployment, the less agent work looks like prompt experimentation and the more it looks like standard engineering governance with new tooling attached.
