## Overview

Simon Willison documented an experiment working with Starlette 1.0 alongside Claude skills. The post was practical rather than corporate: a good example of how developers are actually integrating AI assistance into framework-level exploration and coding tasks.

## Why It Matters For SWE

A lot of the real AI-for-developers story is happening in these smaller workflow experiments. Developers are testing whether skills, structured prompts, and lightweight agent workflows can reduce friction while learning or migrating frameworks.

## Key Signals

- AI skills are entering everyday development workflows.
- Framework exploration is becoming an early proving ground for agent assistance.
- Individual developer practice often previews broader product trends.
- Useful AI tooling does not always require full autonomy to matter.

## SWE Industry Takeaway

The market should not only watch giant product launches. Practical experiments by working developers often reveal where AI assistance is genuinely useful versus where it is just hype.
