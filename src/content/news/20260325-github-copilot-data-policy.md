## Overview

GitHub announced an updated policy under which interaction data from consumer Copilot tiers would be used to train and improve AI models unless users opt out. Business and Enterprise tiers were explicitly excluded from the change.

## Why It Matters For SWE

This is a market and platform governance story as much as a product update. AI coding tools increasingly rely on real-world workflow data to improve performance, but that creates direct tension around privacy, data ownership, and enterprise trust.

## Key Signals

- Training-data policy is now a competitive and product-sensitive issue.
- Enterprise segregation of data remains critical for adoption.
- Real-world usage data is becoming a core differentiator for model quality.
- Developers are being asked to make explicit trade-offs between privacy and product improvement.

## SWE Industry Takeaway

For the AI SWE market, data governance is no longer peripheral. It is becoming part of the core buying and adoption decision for coding assistants and developer platforms.
