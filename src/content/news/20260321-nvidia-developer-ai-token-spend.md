## Overview

The Decoder covered comments from NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang suggesting that highly paid developers should be spending substantial amounts on AI tokens if those tokens increase output. The framing was unapologetically economic: tokens are a labor multiplier.

## Why It Matters For SWE

This is a useful lens on how vendor leaders want enterprises to evaluate AI tooling. Instead of comparing token bills to traditional software budgets, they are comparing them to expensive engineering labor and the speed of delivery.

## Key Signals

- AI spending is being justified in productivity terms, not just experimentation.
- Token economics are becoming part of engineering strategy.
- Infrastructure vendors want higher sustained AI consumption by developers.
- Cost debates are shifting from per-call pricing to return on developer time.

## SWE Industry Takeaway

As AI becomes embedded in engineering workflows, budget conversations will increasingly look like staffing and capital-allocation conversations. The key challenge will be proving the productivity gains are real.
