GitHub product management is actively investigating solutions to address the rapidly growing influx of low-quality, AI-generated project submissions—often referred to as "AI slop." The unsustainable volume of these poor-quality Pull Requests (PRs) has become a critical operational challenge, overwhelming open-source maintainers and threatening the collaborative spirit of the platform.

## Proposed Emergency Measures

In response to growing community backlash, GitHub is considering a series of drastic measures to restore balance:

*   **The "Kill Switch":** Allowing maintainers to completely disable public pull requests for a repository, or strictly restrict them only to trusted and verified collaborators.
*   **Granular Triage Tools:** Introducing the ability to explicitly delete or hide unwanted PRs from view, alongside far more granular permission settings for who can create and review submissions.
*   **AI-Powered Attribution & Filtering:** Ironically, GitHub may use AI to fight AI by deploying automated triage tools to filter out recognizable "slop." They are also exploring transparency mechanics that would explicitly signal when an AI agent or LLM interface was used to generate a submission.

## An Existential Threat to Open Source

Since the aggressive rollout of autonomous coding assistants and mainstream LLM tools, the barrier to entry for contributing code has completely vanished. However, this has resulted in maintainers spending the bulk of their time auditing and dismissing hallucinated, inefficient, or entirely broken code—time previously spent on functional development and community building.

The goal of GitHub's current discussions is to formulate immediate tactical defenses for maintainers while establishing a long-term strategy that preserves open-source collaboration inside an AI-assisted ecosystem.
