For decades, software engineering in Big Tech has been synonymous with stability and high starting salaries. But according to Boris Cherny, the creator of Anthropic's highly viral AI tool **Claude Code**, the job title as we know it could be completely gone by the end of this year.

## Everyone Becomes a Product Manager

Claude Code, an agentic AI designed to autonomously execute complex coding tasks, has seen massive adoption since its release. Unlike standard chatbot interfaces, it can seamlessly manage large codebases.

"I think by the end of the year, everyone is going to be a product manager, and everyone codes. The title software engineer is going to start to go away," Cherny noted during a recent podcast appearance. "It’s just going to be replaced by ‘builder,’ and it’s going to be painful for a lot of people."

To underscore the tool's effectiveness, Cherny revealed that Claude Code has written 100% of his code since November, completely removing his need to write code by hand—though he still reviews the output to ensure safety and correctness.

## The Printing Press Metaphor

Cherny likens the advent of AI autonomous coding to the invention of the printing press. Before the press, scribes painstakingly copied books by hand. When the press democratized reading and writing, scribes were freed up to focus on higher-level creative efforts like bookbinding and illustration.

In Cherny's view, removing the "tedious work" of manual coding allows developers to focus on what actually matters: talking to users, architecting big systems, and designing the future. 

With Anthropic's recent launch of *Cowork*—a user-friendly autonomous coding product tailored to non-technical disciplines—the company expects the automation of standard computer work to stretch globally.

## How to Survive the Disruption

While Anthropic executives acknowledge the massive disruption and potential pain this shift will cause in the labor market, the pace of technology is not slowing down. Cherny offered concrete advice for engineers navigating the transition:

1. **Don't Be Scared**: "Experiment with the tools, get to know them... Just dive in, try them, be on the bleeding edge."
2. **Become a Generalist**: The most rewarded professionals over the next few years will cross over multiple disciplines. Engineers who also understand design, infrastructure, and business strategy will have a distinct advantage.

As AI takes over syntax and logic implementation, the defining trait of a successful tech worker will increasingly become their curiosity and their ability to think about the broader problem they are solving.
